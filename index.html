About me

I'm a second year Mathematical Computation Student at UCL (read computer science and maths), and I've been making websites commercially for around a year now

I like everything to do with programming, I like no-nonsense languages like C, and am quite proficient at front-end web technologies such as javascript, Sass/CSS and using frameworks (Bootstrap/Foundation to Express.js to Laravel 4 PHP).

Automating things is the way to go for me, why would I have to update an entry when I can just give the end user the tools to do it himself? Well you always have to keep in mind that those kinds of abilities come at a price ie. breaking things but nonetheless, make it user-friendly and safe from an admin point of view and that's more free time to do things the end-user wouldn't be able to do

This is also the reason why I love task runners like Grunt, it's sometimes a bit of a slog to set up but once it's done, well... You don't have to worry about running commands manually ~2 million times (for example compiling sass)

More work, less code is the idea: use plugins but know that if you needed to you, you could write it yourself
